Overview

Originating in England in 16th century, cricket emerged into one of popular sports worldwide, particularly in countries like India, Australia, England, Pakistan, South Africa. Cricket is played between two teams of eleven players each on a circular or oval-shaped field. The center of the field features a rectangular 22-yard-long pitch with wickets (three vertical stumps topped by two bails) at each end.

Match is divided into innings, where one team bats while the other team bowls and fields. The primary objective for the batting team is to score runs, while the bowling and fielding team aims to dismiss the batsmen and limit the runs scored. A run is scored when the batsmen successfully run between the wickets after hitting the ball. Dismissals can occur in various ways, such as being bowled, caught, run out, or leg before wicket (LBW).
